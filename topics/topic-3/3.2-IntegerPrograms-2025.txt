Complete LP Characterizations
Complete LP Characterizations
Such complete LP characterizations of combinatorial optimization
problems by linear programs are rarely known,
e.g. for optimization problems like MST or largest forests or minimum
weight perfect matchings or also for special classes of graphs for more
general problems, like bipartite graphs.
However, if these are known, then they offer helpful alternative
(polynomial) solution methods (as opposed to purely combinatorial
algorithms) for the respective optimization problems and also their
variants.

Conclusion
COPs as ILPs
Every combinatorial optimization problem can be formulated as a
Binary Linear Program (BLP)
Integer Linear Programs (ILP) are NP-hard...
COPs as LPs
Every combinatorial optimization problem can even be formulated as
a Linear Program (LP).
However, in general, this description cannot be calculated in
polynomial time and may also be exponentially large.
LPs can be solved in polynomial time.
